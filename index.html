Effective Negotiations

BY PROF. PERRY ATWAL
  • Date: September 14 & 28, 2021
  • Time: 12 - 2 PM
  • Venue: NYU Zoom
Register Brochure

Description

Negotiating skills are essential for managers to achieve their goals. Managers negotiate every day with a variety of stakeholders, including their employers, supervisors, direct reports, vendors, and suppliers.

The goal of this hands‐on two‐part workshop series is to help develop the negotiation skills needed to meet their organizational challenges.

Storytelling to Land Your Job and Grant

BY MS. DASHA RETTEW
  • Date: October 12 & 26, 2021
  • Time: 12 - 2 PM
  • Venue: NYU Zoom
Register Brochure

Description

Ever get the interview question, “Tell me about yourself” and wonder where to begin?

In this two‐part series, by learning about the science and structure of storytelling, which is grounded in the science of emotional intelligence, you will build emotional agility. Next, you will dive deeper into three PhD specific strategies—the elevator pitch, story formula, and “hook”—to tell your story with confidence, land the right job for you, or secure grants where interviews are part of that process.

Decision Making and Leadership

BY PROF. JUSTIN G. BULL
  • Date: November 9 & 23, 2021
  • Time: 12 - 2 PM
  • Venue: NYU Zoom
Register Brochure

Description

In a two‐part workshop series, students will be exposed to common traps in decision‐making and offer frameworks and strategies that will assist in preparing more effective and informed choices. In the first session, students will review common pitfalls in decision making, evaluate mitigation techniques, and have the opportunity to apply these learnings to examples from their own lives and careers.

In the second session, students will work together to identify how to more effectively manage decision making in complex, high‐stress environments.
